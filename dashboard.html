How Does the Typical Sales Funnel Look?
The dominant funnel pattern across 233 agencies. The market has converged on a predictable 4-stage motion.
HOOK — Attract Attention
▼Clutch reviews (127) • SEO/content (87) • LinkedIn (71) • Case studies (50)
ENTRY — First Engagement
▼Free consultation (40) • Lead magnet (14) • Free AI audit (8) • Website chatbot (5)
QUALIFY — Scope & Propose
▼Custom proposal / SOW (88) • Discovery workshop • Requirements gathering
CLOSE — Convert to Revenue
Paid pilot / PoC (22) • Fixed-scope project (39) • Direct retainer (5)

Insight: The funnel is nearly identical everywhere: Clutch reviews → free consult → custom SOW → project. Agencies that break this pattern (paid audits, fixed-scope pilots, transparent pricing) stand out immediately.

Section 05
The Pattern That Separates Winners from Noise

Weak players sell abstractions

"AI Strategy & Transformation"
"AI Readiness Assessment"
"Custom AI Agents" (vague)
"End-to-End AI Solutions"
"Digital Transformation"
94 companies (40%) offer "AI consulting" — the most commoditized service in the dataset. No differentiation, no measurable outcome, no urgency.

Strong players sell pain relief

"Respond to every lead in under 2 minutes"
"Cut manual invoice processing by 80%"
"Stop losing leads after business hours"
"Automate your 2 most expensive manual processes in 30 days"
"Book more appointments from existing inbound"
These agencies close faster, command higher prices, and retain clients longer. The buyer understands the value in 15 seconds.
The core finding from 233 companies: Businesses don't buy AI. They buy outcomes. The market rewards specificity over capability. The best first offer is not "we do AI" — it's "we solve this one expensive problem you have right now, and we do it in 2 weeks."
